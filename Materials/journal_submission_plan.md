# Journal submission plan - free-first strategy

Verified June 2026. Goal: a degree-qualifying, first-author, SCIE-indexed paper at ZERO cost to the author (Anh Tuan Quan, Taipei Medical University).

## Headline finding
There is NO diamond/platinum open-access route left in this niche. Every "diamond OA" candidate now charges an APC. The Journal of Integrative Bioinformatics (the textbook free option for ~20 years) introduced a ~1,050 EUR APC on 1 Feb 2025; Genomics Proteomics and Bioinformatics moved to Oxford and now charges 3,500 USD. Do not trust "diamond OA" labels on aggregator lists.

Two genuine $0 paths exist:
- A. ACS-TMU full APC waiver: publish open access in ANY ACS journal at 0 USD (read-and-publish agreement). Gives free AND open.
- B. Elsevier hybrid via the subscription/non-OA route: 0 USD but the published version stays paywalled (you may self-archive the accepted manuscript after a ~12-month embargo).

## Tier C - NOT free (do not pursue for the $0 goal)
- Scientific Reports, BMC Bioinformatics, BioData Mining, Biology Direct: best scope/policy fit (impact-agnostic "sound science", explicitly negative-result friendly) BUT Springer/BMC gives TMU only a 15% discount, not a waiver - still thousands of USD.
- GigaByte (485 USD AND ESCI-only, not SCIE), F1000Research (not in Web of Science Core), PeerJ Computer Science (ESCI): fail the SCIE requirement.
- PLOS Computational Biology, Bioinformatics, Briefings in Bioinformatics: mandatory APC and/or high novelty desk-screen (likely desk-reject a no-new-method cautionary paper).

## Recommended submission order
1. Journal of Proteome Research (ACS) - FIRST CHOICE. The only option that is both genuinely $0 (ACS-TMU full waiver, open access) AND a strong proteogenomics/methods scope fit at SCIE Q2.
2. Journal of Biomedical Informatics (Elsevier, non-OA) - strong Q1 backup; frame as a data-integration methodology lesson.
3. Computational Biology and Chemistry (Elsevier, non-OA) - safe, lowest desk-reject, methods-oriented Q2.
4. ACS Omega (ACS) - free-and-open fallback, low desk-reject, tolerant of sound-but-not-novel work.
5. Genomics (Elsevier, non-OA) - final backup; re-lead with the positive content.

## Framing that raises odds everywhere
Lead with the POSITIVE deliverables (the cross-validated HCC-iCCA lineage axis, the ground-truth calibration of the anchor check, the clinically-anchored LAT1 dependency) and present non-identifiability as the rigorous lesson, NOT as a bare negative result. This clears the significance/novelty desk-screens that would otherwise reject a pure negative paper. The cover letter already does this; keep it.
