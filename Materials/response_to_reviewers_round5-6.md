# Response to Reviewers - c1 manuscript (panel rounds 5 and 6)

This document records the point-by-point responses to the two most recent simulated review-panel rounds (Round 5: major revision; Round 6: minor revision, leading to "accept with minor revision"). It is written in journal-response format so it can be reused directly when the real reviewer reports arrive. Every number below was regenerated by the released scripts and verified against the deposited stat files; no statistic was hand-edited. Line references are to the revised manuscript.

We thank the editor and reviewers. The panel identified problems that materially improved the paper; we have addressed all of them, and where a fix exposed an awkward number we report it openly rather than select the convenient one.

## ROUND 5 (Major Revision) - eight major points

### M1. The ground-truth titration's headline statistic could be a circular, pseudoreplicated artifact (Reviewers 1, 3, Devil's Advocate)
**Agreed and addressed by both a statistical fix and full disclosure.** The 220 points are 20 replicates nested within each of 11 aliasing levels; we now report the correlation three ways (Methods; Results; Figure 6). (1) Pooled across the 220 points: Spearman -0.68. (2) A level-block bootstrap that resamples the 11 levels (the correct clustering unit) gives a 95 percent CI of -0.72 to -0.43, replacing the anti-conservative iid interval. (3) The mean within-level (alpha-conditioned) Spearman is essentially nil (-0.02 additive, -0.07 multiplicative). We state plainly that the noisy anchor shares the injected-truth term with the recovered effect by construction, so the titration calibrates the SENSITIVITY of a standard check across aliasing regimes, not an independent per-replicate association. The per-level anchor concordance shifts only from 0.105 to 0.042. We do not over-read the pooled value.

### M2. The "harmonization trap" only defeats naive normalizations; a real batch corrector (ComBat/RUV) was cited but never run (Reviewer 1)
**Agreed and implemented.** We added ComBat baselines (Methods; Results). With batch set to cohort and no protected covariate, ComBat erases the contrast (discriminant AUC 0.50, no proteins significant, anchor rho -0.13), exactly like per-cohort z-scoring. Protecting tumor type is ill-posed under perfect aliasing: the design that pairs cohort batch with the tumor-type covariate is rank-deficient (rank 2 of 3 columns), the configuration that the confounded-design guards in sva and pyComBat reject. Forcing a solution through the pseudo-inverse does not recover the batch-free axis (AUC 0.51, 2 percent significant). Reference 13 (ComBat) is now cited in the body. See also Round 6, item R6-1, on the forced-solution anchor concordance.

### M3. The negative proteome-versus-RNA rho was asserted to be a "normalization artifact" rather than shown to be (Reviewers 2, 3)
**Agreed; softened and demonstrated.** We computed the proteome iCCA-minus-HCC direction under four standard normalizations and correlated each with the batch-free RNA axis: raw -0.21, within-sample percentile -0.15, quantile-merge -0.13, per-cohort z-score -0.004. The sign and magnitude move with the normalization choice, so we now describe the negative correlation as harmonization-dependent and uninterpretable as biology rather than naming a specific mechanism (Results).

### M4. The cross-validated AUC used non-nested feature selection and scaling (Reviewer 3)
**Agreed and implemented.** The 2,000-most-variable-gene selection and the scaler are now re-fit inside each fold via an sklearn Pipeline, so the held-out fold informs neither selection nor scaling. The nested CV-AUC is 0.966 (it rose slightly from the non-nested 0.964, so the axis is robust to proper nesting), non-marker 0.966, permutation null 95th percentile 0.629 (Methods; Results).

### M5. The HBV-etiology and tumor-grade confounds were invoked for the proteome but not propagated to the lineage axis or the LAT1 claim (Reviewer 2)
**Agreed and implemented.** We now state that TCGA-LIHC is etiologically mixed and that we did not adjust for tumor grade or proliferation, so part of the iCCA-versus-HCC amino-acid-transport differential could reflect those covariates, and we extend the caveat to the lineage score itself (Results; Limitations item 6).

### M6. The refuted "cross-tumor proteogenomic kinase-activity manifold" is a strawman with no published target; the "kinase" label and "across two cancers" overreach (Devil's Advocate; Reviewers 1, 2)
**Agreed and reframed.** We now present the work as a PROSPECTIVE cautionary benchmark, stating that we know of no published study that merged these two cohorts across tumor type and read the aliased structure as biology, and that it was our own initial direction (Background; Discussion). We qualify the "kinase" label (the enzyme set includes transporters, adaptors, and pseudokinases) and bound "across two cancers" to a powered liver case plus an explicitly unpowered kidney case (232 versus 16 samples, TMT versus label-free).

### M7. The LAT1 leave-out collapse stitched two non-co-generated runs (Integrity officer)
**Agreed and fixed.** The full-set and leave-out competitive-permutation values now come from a single seeded run over a common background (scripts 80 and 81 share the seed and gene universe). The chain is full-set P = 0.0024, then P = 0.20 after removing the LAT1 discovery genes (set-mean delta Chronos -0.059 to -0.011). The selection-independent expression leg survives the same drop (P = 1.6e-10, 83 percent of remaining genes iCCA-up, a figure now computed and stored).

### M8. Figure 2 title contradicted the text; a stale titration CSV shipped; scripts were not portable (Reviewer 1)
**Agreed and implemented.** The Figure 2 panel now reads "Proteome direction is discordant with the batch-free axis." The stale v1 titration CSV was deleted. Scripts were made path-portable (completed fully in Round 6; see R6-2).

### Follow-up items
- **Nanvuranlat hazard ratio.** We replaced "approximately 0.56" with the reported value: HR 0.56 (95 percent CI 0.34 to 0.90, P = 0.02), Furuse 2024, reference 16.
- **Abstract length.** Trimmed from 497 to under 350 words to meet structured-abstract limits, with all reviewer-relevant numbers retained.
- **Inline-number traceability.** Previously unstored inline numbers are now emitted to results/manuscript_inline_numbers.json. In doing so we found that the per-cohort z-score "discriminant AUC 0.506" is the MARKER-score discriminant (0.50); a full multivariate classifier still separates the z-scored cohorts at CV-AUC 0.975. We now disclose both and frame the residual separability as the batch artifact we decline to read as biology (Results).

## ROUND 6 (Minor Revision; outcome "accept with minor revision") - two must-fix items plus minors

### R6-1. The forced-pinv ComBat protect-tumor-type branch stores a positive anchor rho (+0.18) that the text omitted (Reviewers 1, 2, 3)
**Agreed; this was the one selective-reporting lapse and we have corrected it.** The text now reports the +0.18 anchor concordance alongside the AUC 0.51 and interprets it: protecting a covariate that is perfectly aliased with batch forces ComBat to re-inject the tumor-type contrast, manufacturing apparent concordance that is the aliased artifact itself, not recovered signal (Results). This turns the omitted number into a confirmation of the identifiability thesis.

### R6-2. The "all scripts path-portable" claim was false; the kidney script crashed as shipped (Reviewer 1)
**Agreed and fully fixed.** All analysis scripts are now path-portable (each resolves its directory from its own location); no script contains a hardcoded path. We verified that the kidney-generality script regenerates its numbers (76 percent of proteins significant, anchor rho 0.004). We added scripts/run_all.sh as a single top-level reproduction runner, so "a top-level script regenerates the stat files" is now literally true.

### Round 6 minor points
- **Abstract over-read of -0.68.** The abstract now carries the regime-level qualifier ("across aliasing regimes ... a between-regime not per-replicate trend"), so the headline is not read as per-replicate diagnostic power.
- **EGFR BH-FDR.** The value 0.625 (reported as 0.63) is now emitted to the inline-number JSON rather than stored as null.
- **z-score full-LDA separability.** Reported as 0.975 (not rounded up to 0.98) to match the released number.
- **ComBat abort wording.** Softened from "standard implementations halt" to a property of the confounded-design guards in sva and pyComBat.

## Summary of the two rounds
Round 5 (major) resolved eight construct-validity and reproducibility problems and three follow-ups; Round 6 (minor) closed the two residual issues a revision can introduce (one selective-reporting lapse and an incomplete reproducibility claim). The internal panel score rose from 68 to 84 of 100, and the final integrity pass confirmed that all 38 load-bearing numbers reproduce from the released scripts with no stale survivors. The manuscript now stands on a non-circular core (the harmonization trap and the ComBat impossibility result), an honestly bounded calibration, a vetted lineage axis, and a small clinically anchored LAT1 signal.
